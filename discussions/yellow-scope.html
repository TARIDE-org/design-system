Decided · 18 May 2026 · yellow scope

Yellow = verified credential, only.

Option A is locked. Below is the original discussion document, preserved for the record. The decision block at the bottom is updated; my recommendation (Option B) was overridden in favour of the tighter scope, which keeps yellow at maximum attention-weight on the one surface that matters.

Option A · tightest

Verified credential only

Rule. Yellow appears only where the protocol or UI surfaces a verified credential — the pill indicator and the framed credential card. Nothing else in the system uses yellow.

Yellow appears on

  • The verified-credential pill: Verified · kpn.nl
  • The framed credential card (issuer / scope / chain)
  • Marginalia, notes, callouts — these use blue or grey
  • "Rule:" annotations in diagrams — these would need re-tinting
  • Quotes, highlights, pull-out asides

Frequency on a typical page

~1 of 10 surfaces. Yellow is rare → high attention when it appears. Brand-distinctive.

Option B · mid · my recommendation

Attestation — wherever something is being asserted

Rule. Yellow marks attestation — anywhere a party (the protocol, the foundation, an operator, a human reviewer) asserts something to be true: verified credentials, rule/constraint annotations, signed quotes, foundation-board statements, attested data points.

Yellow appears on

  • Verified-credential pill + framed card (Option A's surfaces)
  • "Rule:" annotations in diagrams (already the existing convention in foundation-doc SVGs)
  • Foundation-asserted statements ("the board has resolved…")
  • Attested data points (a stat with a verified-source citation marker)
  • Signed quotes from named individuals
  • Decorative use, highlights, hover states

Frequency on a typical page

~3 of 10 surfaces. Still scarce, but the brand reads as an attestation system from any single screen.

Option C · loosest

Co-equal accent — yellow alongside blue

Rule. Yellow stands on equal footing with blue as a system accent. Used for secondary actions, attention-pulls, highlights — anywhere a designer would reach for "the other brand colour".

Yellow appears on

  • Everything Option B covers
  • Secondary buttons / alternative actions
  • Highlights and pull-quotes
  • Section dividers and rules where contrast is wanted
  • Hovered link states (matched-pair with blue links)

Frequency on a typical page

~6 of 10 surfaces. Maximum brand recognition; the yellow-bracket logo variant is no longer alternate but co-canonical.

decided · option A

Yellow appears only on the verified-credential pill and the framed credential card. No marginalia, no annotations, no rules-in-diagrams, no secondary buttons. The bracket-frame motif (corner registration marks) and the dot-with-halo indicator carry attestation visually without colour.

Downstream effects: (i) the foundation-document diagrams' "Rule:" annotations are recoloured to signal-blue, not yellow. (ii) signed-quote and foundation-rule components, had they existed under Option B, are not built. (iii) yellow's brand-recognition role rests entirely on the credential surfaces — they earn their weight.
